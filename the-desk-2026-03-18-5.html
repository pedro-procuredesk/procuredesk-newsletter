The STOP-MRO Framework: Fix How Your Team Buys Maintenance, Repair & Operations

Most MRO purchasing runs on habit, not process. This webinar shows you how to change that — without a six-month implementation.

📅 Tuesday, March 25  ·  2 PM EST

MRO is the category CFOs most often underestimate. It's not the biggest line item — but it's the most uncontrolled one. Purchases happen across departments. Vendors multiply. Stock levels are guessed, not managed. And by the time someone notices the problem, a line has already gone down or a budget has already blown past.

"The issue isn't that teams spend too much on MRO. It's that nobody can see what's being spent, by whom, or why — until it's too late."

The STOP-MRO Framework is a practical, four-part approach to getting MRO under control without overhauling your entire procurement stack. In 30 minutes, you'll walk away with a clear picture of where your biggest leaks are and a concrete starting point for fixing them.

S
Standardize
Define approved vendors, part numbers, and reorder points by category
  
T
Track
Build real-time visibility into stock levels, open POs, and spend by location
O
Optimize
Consolidate vendors, reduce emergency orders, and negotiate on volume
  
P
Protect
Set approval rules that prevent maverick spend before it happens

● From the CEO's Desk
S

Sachin Sharma

Founder & CEO, ProcureDesk

Compliance doesn't slow businesses down. Manual signatures do. I regularly talk to construction and manufacturing teams trying to tighten controls by adding a second or third approval layer to every purchase. But if it already takes days to approve a material requisition, an extra sign-off typically creates more shadow ops in the field as crews find workarounds to keep production moving. True compliance is a byproduct of a clean process. Fix the cycle time first, then automate the guardrails so accuracy becomes automatic.

Stampli vs. Bill.com: Which One Is Actually Built for Mid-Market AP?

Both are widely used. Both automate invoice processing. But they're built for different companies — and picking the wrong one creates more work, not less.

Feature Stampli Bill.com
Best fit Mid-market, complex workflows Small business, simple AP/AR
ERP integration 70+ ERPs, full native support Limited — adapts to their model
Vendor payments Pay inside or outside Pushes vendors to their network
Pricing Custom quote only Published tiers
AR automation Not included Yes — combined AP + AR

Neither platform connects purchasing to AP. If your team is still managing POs in spreadsheets and reconciling them to invoices manually, the bottleneck is upstream — not in the AP tool.
