📊  New Dashboard  ·  Available Now

Cost Avoidance Dashboard

Every rejected requisition is money that didn't leave the company. Now you can see exactly how much — and put a number on what your procurement controls are worth.

Dashboards  ›  New Tab

Most procurement teams know their approval workflows are working — but they can't prove it. When finance asks "what is this system actually saving us?", the honest answer has been "we're not sure." The Cost Avoidance Dashboard changes that. It tracks the total value of rejected requisitions and shows you, clearly, what your controls prevented from being spent.

"Your approval process isn't overhead. The Cost Avoidance Dashboard is how you prove it's an asset."

The dashboard is available now as a new tab under Dashboards. Filter savings by department, category, approver, vendor, or requester. Drill into any rejected requisition to see the count, value, and who was involved. No configuration needed — it pulls from your existing approval data automatically.

● Set It Up This Week

5 things you can activate before Friday

These features are live in your account. Most take under 10 minutes to enable. Here's where to start.

01 Open the Cost Avoidance Dashboard. Go to Dashboards and look for the new tab. No setup needed — it already pulls from your existing approval data. Worth sharing the link with your CFO directly.
02 Try receipt scanning on mobile. Open the ProcureDesk app, go to the expense list, and tap the upload icon. Point your camera at a receipt — the system extracts the vendor, amount, and date and suggests a match from your existing drafts.
03 Check the Cashflow Forecasting Dashboard. Also in Dashboards under a new tab. It combines unpaid invoices, receipts without invoices, open PO obligations, and non-PO spend trends into a single 3–6 month forward view.
04 Configure Parallel Approvals for cross-functional workflows. In your workflow configuration page, you'll find Parallel Approval as a new step type in the dropdown. Most useful for any workflow currently routing Finance → IT → Legal in sequence.
05 Set Self-Approval Limits for users with high approval authority. In User Profile, uncheck "Use Authorization Limit for Self-Approval" and enter a separate threshold. Recommended for any user who creates and approves their own requisitions.

The rest of what shipped this month

Three more updates — each one changes how a specific part of the workflow behaves.

📷

Mobile  ·  Expenses

OCR for Expense Creation

Upload a receipt from your phone camera or gallery. OCR extracts vendor, amount, and date — then suggests the matching draft expense from your credit card feed. No more manually hunting for the right expense line. Watch the walkthrough →
📅

Purchasing  ·  Requests

Delayed Release for Requests

Get a request approved once. Then release orders from it whenever you need them — partially, in full, or across multiple releases. Ideal for recurring purchases or planned procurement spread across a fiscal year. Enable the toggle when creating a new request.
📈

Finance  ·  Dashboards

Cashflow Forecasting Dashboard

Combines unpaid invoices, receipts without invoices, open PO obligations, and non-PO spend trends into a single 3–6 month forward view. Designed so finance teams can plan liquidity without waiting for accounting to close the books. Available now as a new tab under Dashboards.
